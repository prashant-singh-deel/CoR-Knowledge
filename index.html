Product Overview

Deel Contractor of Record (COR) is an Agency of Record model in which Deel hires independent contractors on behalf of the client and manages the full contractor lifecycle: classification, compliance, contracting, invoicing, and payment. Clients get access to a global, flexible workforce without taking on the legal risk of contractor misclassification.

Renamed August 2024 "Deel Shield" is now officially "Deel Contractor of Record (COR)." All references, including internal and external comms, should use the new name.

How It Works — In One Sentence

The client selects COR when creating a contractor contract → Deel conducts an eligibility check → if approved, Deel signs a contract with the contractor → Deel invoices the client a management fee + contractor compensation → Deel assumes all misclassification liability.

Availability & Eligibility

General Rule COR is available in any country where Deel supports Independent Contractor (IC) hiring.

Geographic Restrictions

California (US) — PROHIBITED COR is not permitted under any circumstances in California.
United States — Case-by-Case Approval COR in the US requires case-by-case approval regardless of where the client or contractor is located. Significant misclassification risk applies.

Key Rule: Entity Country ≠ Contractor Country Clients cannot hire a COR contractor via a legal entity in the same country where the contractor is based.

Example: A client with a UK entity must use their Irish entity to hire a COR contractor based in the UK.

Customer Onboarding (OBM & CSM)

All COR onboarding follows the OBM process. Two paths are available:

Path A — Individual Contract Creation (Client Self-Service)

  1. Client creates a new contractor contract (Fixed / PAYG / Milestone)
  2. At the Compliance step, selects "Full Protection by Deel"
  3. Completes eligibility assessment
  4. Contract submitted to Deel for approval (auto or manual)
  5. Fixed contracts: client pays deposit
  6. Contractor receives email invitation
  7. Contractor completes onboarding: KYC → RTW (if required) → mandatory documents
  8. Client signs SOW (Exhibit A)
  9. Deel countersigns → contract goes active

Path B — Mass Onboarding (10+ Contractors)

  1. OBM/CSM sends the COR Onboarding Mass Template to the client
  2. Client populates the template
  3. OBM/CSM imports in Admin: Client account → Contracts → Import
  4. Select team, client org & legal entity (Deel Inc)
  5. Upload the TSV file
  6. Follow remaining individual steps
COR contracts can reside on any Team. No need to add deel-shield@deel.com as admin — this is now automated.

SOW Manual Review Steps (GSC ONB Team)

  1. Add 🟡 emoji to Slack notification (prevents duplicate work)
  2. Check the reason for the approval request
  3. Open contract via the "Approval request" link
  4. Log in as Provider with write access (Deel)
  5. Update contract as needed (remove additional docs, remove client name from SOW, etc.)
  6. Verify all components against compliance requirements
  7. Click Approve & Sign
  8. Update emoji to ✅
  9. Tag CSM in thread with changes made

Deposit Refund Process

Upon termination, the deposit status moves to "Pending Refund." Refund is processed once:

  • The client has paid the final invoice on the contract
  • The client has provided bank details for the refund

Clients view deposit status under the "Deposits" card on the COR Contract Details page.

Worked Example — 15% with $325 Minimum (PAYG)

Invoice 1:
Work: $1,000 | 15% = $150 → Below minimum → Client pays $325

Invoice 2:
Work: $1,000 | 15% of month total ($2,000) = $300 → Still below minimum (already paid $325) → $0 charged

Invoice 3:
Work: $1,000 | 15% of month total ($3,000) = $450 → Above minimum → $450 - $325 already paid = $125 charged

All pricing changes (including deposits) must go through GSC / Deal Desk for approval. Partner / YC discounts are NOT available for COR.

  • All invoices issued from Deel, Inc. (US entity)

Compliance Documents

Missing documents do NOT block contracts or payments. Compliance documents serve only as a risk-reduction checklist. Contractors are still able to get paid and withdraw funds even if documents are outstanding.

Individual vs. Business/Entity

COR contractors can onboard as individuals or businesses/entities depending on country and how they operate. Since COR is a contractor model, the contractor is responsible for their own tax, social security, invoicing, and accounting.

Important for Support Teams Do NOT state "no business setup is needed" as a blanket rule. Requirements vary by country. Always check the Contractor Compliance Docs Dashboard for country-specific requirements.

In many countries, contractors must have a recognized structure such as: self-employed / freelancer registration, sole proprietorship, professional / freelance license, or an LLC / limited company.

FAQs & Common Scenarios

Eligibility & Product

Can COR contractors be individuals or business entities? ▼
Yes. COR contractors can be onboarded as either individuals or businesses/entities depending on their country and how they operate. Since COR is a contractor model, the contractor remains responsible for their own tax and compliance obligations.
Can the COR contractor be in the same country as the client? ▼
Possible via an exemption, but not the default. Requires a questionnaire and Legal review. Not automatically approved.
How do we know if a worker should be COR vs. EOR? ▼
COR is for workers legitimately operating as independent contractors. If the actual working relationship more closely resembles employment (exclusive engagement, set hours, company tools, etc.), EOR is likely more appropriate. The 2-question eligibility assessment helps determine this — "No" on Question 1 typically suggests EOR is better.
Can interns or C-suite be hired via COR? ▼
Interns: No, not eligible for COR. C-suite (Director+): Not by default, but an exemption can be requested via Legal approval.
Can COR contracts be transferred across legal entities? ▼
No. COR contracts cannot be transferred across legal entities.

Contracts & Signing

Can clients redline the contractor contract? ▼
No. Clients cannot make any changes or redlines to the standard Deel contractor contract used for COR.
What if the contractor signs after the contract start date? ▼
COR contracts cannot be backdated. The client must update the start date to the signing date or later, and both parties must re-sign the contract.
Does the SOW need to be signed for every COR contract? ▼
Yes. The client must actively sign the SOW (Exhibit A) before Deel can countersign. This applies to every COR contract.
Can a client use one EOR/COR MSA to hire on a different legal entity? ▼
No. A separate MSA must be signed per legal entity. An MSA on one entity cannot be reused for a different entity.

Deposits & Pricing

Why is a deposit charged for fixed contracts? ▼
Fixed rate contracts commit Deel to paying the contractor a set amount monthly regardless of whether the client pays Deel first. The deposit protects Deel in case the client fails to pay.
I see a deposit on a PAYG/Milestone contract. Is this correct? ▼
No. Only Fixed Rate contracts require deposits. If a PAYG or Milestone contract is requesting a deposit, escalate to #product-issues as this may be a platform bug.

Invoicing & Payments

What is the difference between Management Fees and Consulting Fees? ▼
Consulting Fee: The contractor's compensation (salary, hours, milestone payment). Invoiced from the contractor to Deel, then mirrored to the client.

Management Fee: Deel's service fee for COR coverage. Either 15% of the consulting fee or $325/month — whichever is higher.
Does Automatic Payment work with COR? ▼
Yes. Auto-billing for COR invoices became available for all clients as of May 2025. Configurable in Billing & Payments settings.
If a client brings on a COR contractor in a non-US country, which Deel entity handles it? ▼
All COR contracts go through Deel Inc (the US entity), regardless of the contractor's country of location.

Compliance & Tax

Does Deel fully take on all misclassification risk? ▼
Deel assumes liability for its classification decision and indemnifies clients against legally enforceable misclassification claims. However, this indemnity is contingent on a definitive payment order or reclassification determination by a competent authority. It is not a blanket guarantee against all possible risk scenarios.

Termination

Can the client terminate a COR contractor immediately? ▼
If the client pays an amount equal to or greater than what is owed (including notice period coverage), they can terminate without requiring an MTA. Otherwise, the notice period in the contract must be honored (must be more than 1 day).
Who notifies the contractor that their contract has been terminated? ▼
Deel notifies the contractor via email once the client has selected the contract end date. The client does not directly communicate termination to the contractor.
Can I downgrade a COR contract back to a regular IC contract? ▼
Not directly. You must terminate the COR contract and then create a brand new IC contract. There is no in-platform transition flow.

Verification Letters

Can a COR contractor request an Employment Verification Letter? ▼
No. COR contractors are not employees and do not have access to Employment Verification features. They can request a Contractor Verification Letter instead, if enabled by the client in their organization settings.
